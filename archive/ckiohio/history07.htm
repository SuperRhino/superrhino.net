Traditional Club Events


Ohio State's Teeter Totter MarathonBegan: 1973

Traditionally held in May, Ohio State's Tetter Totter Marathon or TTM is the Ohio District's longest running service project dating back to 1973. The 2002 TTM raised more than $2800 for the Make A Wish Foundation setting a new record


University of Dayton Semi-FormalBegan: 1990

Normally held in October, the University of Dayton's Semi-Formal gives Ohio Circle K'ers a chance to dress up and party down! Usually the event is preceeded by a dinner and service project


Walsh University Dance MarathonBegan: 1996

Typically in November, the Walsh University Circle K puts on this massive dancing event. Musical chairs and the Walsh Ice Ice Baby Dance are always included for the enjoyment of all the attendants. Circle K'ers dance for 12 hours to raise money for charities


Miami University Spaghetti DinnerBegan: 1998

This event is usually held within the first couple months of the calendar year. The Miami Circle K hosts an amazing spaghetti dinner and provides entertainment in the form of music, karaoke, and more. The money made from the dinner is donated to charity


Bowling Green State University Game-A-ThonBegan: 1989

Normally held in January, BGSU invites people to come play games, have fun and help raise money for charity. Game-a-thon began in 1989 with a group of students and has since developed into a much larger project
